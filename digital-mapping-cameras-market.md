<p><p>Digital Mapping Cameras (DMC) vary in bit depth, which determines the amount of color information they capture. 8-bit DMCs offer basic color representation, suitable for simple mapping tasks. 10-bit DMCs provide improved color fidelity for detailed landscapes. 12-bit DMCs enhance tonal range, making them ideal for complex environments. 14-bit DMCs offer even greater precision, valuable in professional surveys. 16-bit DMCs capture the highest detail, catering to high-demand applications. Other types may include specialized cameras for unique mapping needs or formats.</p></p>
