<p><p>Restriction endonucleases, enzymes that cut DNA at specific sequences, are categorized into several types based on their structure and function. Type I enzymes are complex and require ATP for activity, cutting DNA far from their recognition site. Type II, the most commonly used in labs, cut DNA at precise sites and do not require ATP. Type III enzymes cut DNA after a recognition sequence and also require ATP. Type IV enzymes target modified DNA, such as methylated DNA, while Other types encompass newly characterized or less common endonucleases.</p></p>
