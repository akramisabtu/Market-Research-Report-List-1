<p><p>Bioceramics are materials designed for medical applications, categorized into bio-inert, bio-active, and bio-resorbable types. Bio-inert ceramics are non-reactive with body tissues, often used in implants. Bio-active ceramics stimulate biological responses and promote bonding with tissues, making them suitable for bone repair. Bio-resorbable ceramics gradually dissolve and get replaced by natural tissue, ideal for temporary implants. Piezoceramics generate electrical charges under mechanical stress, widely used in sensors and actuators in various applications, including medical devices.</p></p>
