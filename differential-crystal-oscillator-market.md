<p><p>In the differential crystal oscillator market, "passive" and "active" types are distinguished by their operation and control mechanisms. Passive oscillators utilize external circuitry to generate oscillations, relying on passive components and offering simplicity and stability. In contrast, active oscillators employ integrated circuitry, providing enhanced performance, frequency stability, and flexibility in design. While passive oscillators are often favored for their cost-effectiveness and low power draw, active oscillators are preferred in high-performance applications requiring precise frequency control.</p></p>
