<p><p>The dyestuff market comprises various types of dyes, each catering to specific applications and substrates. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers like polyester. Reactive dyes bond chemically with textiles, making them ideal for cotton. Sulfur dyes are cost-effective for dyeing cellulose fibers and are known for their washfastness. Vat dyes offer excellent lightfastness and are suitable for cotton and other fibers. Acid dyes are used for protein fibers like wool. Other dyes encompass a range of specialty applications.</p></p>
