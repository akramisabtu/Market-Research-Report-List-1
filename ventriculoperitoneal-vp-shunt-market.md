Fixed pressure valves maintain a constant pressure level, offering consistent cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) drainage, while variable pressure valves allow adaptability to changing intracranial pressure, providing tailored drainage dynamics. These distinctions influence clinical applications, patient outcomes, and surgeon preferences, driving demand in the healthcare market for effective management of conditions like hydrocephalus.</p></p>
