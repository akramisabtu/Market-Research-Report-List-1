<p><p>Wind power systems can be categorized into on-grid and off-grid markets. On-grid systems are connected to the electricity grid, allowing excess energy to be sold back to the utility. This type facilitates grid stability and integrates with other renewable sources. In contrast, off-grid systems operate independently, providing power to remote areas without access to the grid. They typically include battery storage systems to manage supply and demand, ensuring a consistent energy source for standalone applications or smaller communities.</p></p>
