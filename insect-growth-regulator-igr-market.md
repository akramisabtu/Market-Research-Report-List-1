<p><p>Insect Growth Regulators (IGRs) are chemical compounds that disrupt the development and growth of insects. The market can be classified into three key types: </p><p>1. **Chitin Synthesis Inhibitors**: These disrupt the formation of chitin, essential for exoskeleton development, preventing larvae from maturing.</p><p>2. **Juvenile Hormone Analogs & Mimics**: These compounds mimic juvenile hormones, interfering with normal growth and development, keeping insects in their immature stages.</p><p>3. **Anti-Juvenile Hormone Agents**: They counteract juvenile hormones, triggering premature metamorphosis, reducing insect populations by affecting reproductive processes.</p></p>
